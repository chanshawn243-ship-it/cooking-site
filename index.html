Knife Skills

The Chef's Knife Grip

Hold the knife with your thumb and index finger pinching the blade base, while other fingers wrap around the handle. This gives maximum control and safety.

The Claw Hand Technique

Curl your fingertips inward when holding ingredients. Use your knuckles as a guide for the knife blade to prevent cuts.

Rocking Motion Cutting

For herbs and delicate items, use a rocking motion with the knife tip on the cutting board, moving the blade up and down smoothly.

Heat Control Mastery

High Heat: Searing & Sautéing

Used to create a flavorful crust on proteins and vegetables. Oil should shimmer before adding food. Perfect for achieving caramelization.

Medium Heat: The Sweet Spot

Ideal for most everyday cooking. Allows food to cook through evenly while developing flavor without burning.

Low Heat: Gentle Cooking

Perfect for delicate sauces, melting chocolate, and slow-cooking. Prevents overcooking and maintains ingredient integrity.
